What is BMI (Body Mass Index) ?

BMI is a simple calculation used to determine if your weight is appropriate for your height. It is commonly used as a general indicator of healthy body weight.

Formulas:

     BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)2

Categories:

     • Underweight: BMI < 18.5

     • Normal weight: 18.5 ≤ BMI < 24.9

     • Overweight: 25 ≤ BMI < 29.9

     • Obese: BMI ≥ 30

What is BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) ?

BMR is the number of calories your body requires to perform its basic functions, like breathing, circulating blood, and maintaining body temperature, while at complete rest.

Formulas:

     • For Men:

          BMR = 13.7 × weight (kg) + 5.0 × height (cm) − 6.8 × age (years) + 66

     • For Women:

          BMR = 9.6 × weight (kg) + 1.8 × height (cm) − 4.7 × age (years) + 655

What is TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) ?

TDEE is the total number of calories that your body requires in a day in order to maintain your current weight. This is also known as your maintenance level.

Formulas:

     TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor

Activity Factors:

     • Sedentary (little/no exercise): 1.2

     • Lightly active (light exercise/sports 1-3 days/week): 1.375

     • Moderately active (moderate exercise/sports 3-5 days/week): 1.55

     • Very active (hard exercise/sports 6-7 days/week): 1.725

     • Super active (very hard exercise/physical job): 1.9
